# North Downs Line East

The North Downs Line runs from Reading to Redhill along the North Downs, a ridge of hills through Southern England. This simulation covers the eastern section, from Guildford to Redhill.

This line has limited signalling along most its length, meaning trains can catch up with each other quickly. Additionally, there are two level crossings, and it must be ensured that these aren't closed for too long.

## Simulation

A timetable for 31 August 2022 has been included, running from 05:10 until the last train of the evening, at approximately 1am. The standard services are:

- 2tph London Victoria to Reigate (SN)
- 1tph Reading to Gatwick Airport (GW)
- 1tph Reading to Redhill (GW)

Additional peak services from Reading also terminate at Shalford.

## Operational Tips

Keep an eye on departure tumes from Reigate towards Redhill - some trains have extended dwells to ensure they don't leave at the same time.

Be careful not to set routes across level crossings too early, as trains can take longer than expected to travel some sections. The Actions Due panel can be helpful to determine how long until a crossing should be closed.
